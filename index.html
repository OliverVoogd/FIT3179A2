Natural Disasters Around the World 1980-2018

On average, around 60,000 people are killed by natural disasters around the world.

Historically, floods and droughts were the most impactful, however now the most deaths are from earthquakes.

Disasters affect those in lower socioeconomic countries more. Higher deaths tolls tend to be located in such countries, which lack infrastructure to respond to these events.






Over the last decade, around 60,000 people died from natural disasters each year.

Usually, most disaster don't result in many deaths. However, there are low frequency and high impact events, such as the 2010 Haiti earthquake and the drought in Ethiopia in 1983, which cause devasting impacts.
